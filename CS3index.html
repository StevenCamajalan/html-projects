Kaijo High

VS

Fukuda Sogo Academy

First Half

The game begins with Kise on edge. Haizaki’s intimidating, rough style and his ability to "steal" and twist the moves of his opponents disrupt Kise's focus, throwing him off his game. Haizaki taunts Kise, reminding him of his "replacement" status back at Teiko, fueling an aggressive rivalry.Haizaki dominates the first half, using his "Technique Theft" skill to nullify Kaijo’s offense and defense. By degrading Kise’s teammates' techniques and making them his own, Haizaki keeps Kaijo at a disadvantage. This establishes Fukuda Sogo's early lead and leaves Kaijo struggling to keep up.

Second Half

As the game progresses, Kise begins to push through Haizaki's psychological tactics. He recalls his own growth and the value of his team. Fired up, Kise starts to believe in his abilities, unlocking his Perfect Copy ability as he channels the moves of the Generation of Miracles.Kise’s Perfect Copy allows him to imitate moves from top players like Aomine, Midorima, and Murasakibara. Using Aomine’s speed and agility, Midorima’s long-range shooting, and Murasakibara’s strength, he overwhelms Haizaki, regaining control of the game and putting Kaijo back in the lead.Haizaki becomes increasingly desperate, unable to handle Kise’s wide range of new techniques. His attempts to stop Kise with aggressive fouls and taunts fail, as Kise maintains his focus, continuing to masterfully combine moves from the Generation of Miracles.

Final Moments

In the closing minutes, Kise fully embraces his role as a true ace. He uses Perfect Copy to an even greater extent, leaving Haizaki unable to keep up. Kise’s resilience and determination overpower Haizaki, who realizes he can’t break Kise’s spirit or steal his moves effectively.Kise’s performance in the final moments is flawless. With a combination of all his borrowed techniques, he delivers a decisive blow to Fukuda Sogo’s defense, securing the win for Kaijo. This marks a major victory for Kise, who has overcome not only Haizaki’s dirty tactics but also his own doubts and fears.Haizaki, defeated and humiliated, leaves the court in frustration, while Kise is celebrated as a hero by his team. This game signifies Kise’s growth, proving he’s no longer just a “copy” but a player with a unique spirit and strength of his own.

Shutoku High

VS

Rakuzan High

First Half

Rakuzan High takes control of the game from the very start, thanks to Akashi Seijuro's Emperor Eye, which allows him to predict and counter every move by Shutoku. Akashi’s unparalleled court vision and authority over his teammates make Rakuzan's teamwork seem flawless, while Shutoku struggles to keep up.Midorima Shintaro, the sharp-shooting ace of Shutoku, and his partner, Kazunari Takao, attempt to counter Rakuzan’s lead. Takao uses his Hawk Eye to read the court, but Akashi’s Emperor Eye surpasses it, disrupting their offense. Midorima’s three-point shots give Shutoku some points, but they still trail behind as Rakuzan continues to execute plays with ease.Akashi uses his psychological insight to get under Midorima’s skin, reminding him that no matter how many long-range shots he makes, it won’t be enough to change the game’s outcome if Shutoku can’t control the court. This sows doubt among Shutoku players and makes them question their ability to close the gap.

Second Half

Despite the setbacks, Midorima refuses to give up. He starts taking three-pointers from even farther out, pushing his limits to score against Rakuzan. His range and accuracy force Rakuzan to adjust their defense, providing Shutoku with brief scoring opportunities.Akashi adapts quickly, instructing his teammates to focus on preventing Shutoku from getting into their rhythm. The Rakuzan players, individually skilled and physically strong, control both the offense and defense, gradually widening the score gap once again.Takao pushes his own skills to the limit, using his Hawk Eye to assist Midorima’s shots, trying to create openings that might destabilize Rakuzan’s defense. Their teamwork improves Shutoku’s performance, bringing them closer, but Akashi’s constant interference disrupts their flow.

Final Moments

In a last-ditch effort to turn the game around, Midorima makes several audacious three-point shots from beyond his usual range. His dedication and confidence inspire Shutoku’s team, and they rally to close the score gap. Midorima’s relentless shooting tests Rakuzan’s limits and puts immense pressure on Akashi to maintain control.Seeing Midorima’s persistence, Akashi steps up, asserting his dominance and reminding Shutoku of Rakuzan’s superiority. He refines his use of the Emperor Eye, dictating the final plays with absolute precision. By cutting off Shutoku’s scoring attempts and leading his teammates to execute flawless counters, he ends Shutoku’s comeback hopes.The final whistle blows with Rakuzan as the victor, securing their place in the Winter Cup finals. Shutoku falls short despite Midorima’s best efforts, while Akashi’s control over the court reaffirms Rakuzan’s reputation as the top team.

Seirin High

VS

Kaijo High

First Half

Right from the start, Kise activates his Perfect Copy, an advanced skill that allows him to copy and use the abilities of the other Generation of Miracles players for a limited time. With this, Kise becomes a versatile powerhouse, combining Aomine’s agility, Midorima’s shooting range, Murasakibara’s defensive strength, and Akashi’s control over the court.Kise’s overwhelming abilities leave Seirin scrambling to keep up. Kagami and Kuroko try to counter him, but Kise’s near-perfect imitation of the Generation of Miracles gives him a clear advantage. Kaijo takes an early lead, forcing Seirin to adapt their strategy.To combat Kise’s dominance, Kuroko brings out his Phantom Shot, a technique that takes advantage of his misdirection skills to make a seemingly invisible shot. This catches Kise off guard and helps Seirin keep the score close.

Second Half

With Seirin struggling, Kagami enters the "Zone," significantly boosting his speed, agility, and reflexes. His enhanced abilities allow him to go toe-to-toe with Kise, who relies on Perfect Copy. This creates an electrifying back-and-forth between the two aces, as Kagami and Kise trade powerful, dynamic plays.As the game intensifies, Kise’s body starts to feel the toll of Perfect Copy, which requires immense stamina and focus. His moves become less sharp, but he continues to push through the pain, determined to defeat Seirin and prove his strength.Seirin capitalizes on Kise’s exhaustion by reinforcing their teamwork. Kuroko and Kagami combine their strengths, with Kuroko using his misdirection and passing skills to set up scoring opportunities for Kagami. Their coordinated plays keep them in the game and push Kaijo to the edge.

Final Moments

With only a few minutes left, Kise uses Perfect Copy one last time, despite his physical strain. He combines Aomine’s unpredictable movements with Midorima’s long-range shooting, catching Seirin off guard and narrowing the score gap. This final effort creates a nail-biting conclusion to the game.Kagami enters the "True Zone," where he’s able to bring out the best of his teammates rather than playing solo. This allows Seirin to fully unite, each player giving everything they have to keep up with Kise’s relentless offense. With Kuroko’s support and the synergy of the entire team, Kagami manages to break through Kaijo’s defense.In the last seconds, Kuroko and Kagami execute a combined play, leading Kagami to score the game-winning shot. Seirin emerges victorious with a narrow win over Kaijo, advancing to the next round of the Winter Cup.

Seirin High

VS

Rakuzan High

First Half

Rakuzan High quickly takes control of the game, with Akashi using his Emperor Eye to predict Seirin's moves. His Emperor Eye allows him to read his opponents’ movements and intentions perfectly, making him nearly impossible to beat in one-on-one situations. With Akashi leading, Rakuzan pulls ahead, putting immense pressure on Seirin.Seirin initially struggles to counter Akashi’s Emperor Eye. Every time they attempt a play, Akashi anticipates it, and Rakuzan’s players, each highly skilled in their positions, block Seirin from executing their strategies. Hayama Kotaro uses his Lightning Dribble, while Nebuya Eikichi overwhelms with his sheer power, and Reo Mibuchi provides consistent scoring.Kagami and Kuroko realize they need to adapt quickly if they want a chance at victory. Kagami uses his strength and speed to keep up with Akashi, while Kuroko begins using Misdirection Overflow—a technique that makes Kagami the center of attention, amplifying his presence and allowing Kuroko to remain unnoticed. This helps Seirin gain some ground, but they still trail behind Rakuzan.

Second Half

Desperate to keep up with Rakuzan, Kagami enters the “Zone,” significantly boosting his speed, strength, and reflexes. In the Zone, Kagami is able to bypass Rakuzan’s defense and score against them, closing the gap and re-energizing Seirin. His presence disrupts Rakuzan’s rhythm, and Akashi begins to sense that Seirin won’t be defeated easily.Sensing that Kagami’s Zone could shift the game’s balance, Akashi undergoes a transformation. His "true self" awakens, creating a more balanced version of Akashi who not only uses the Emperor Eye but also values the strength of teamwork. This shift makes Rakuzan even more formidable, as Akashi’s evolved tactics allow him to unify and amplify his teammates’ skills.Kagami reaches a higher level known as the "True Zone." In this state, he realizes that the power of the Zone isn’t just about individual skill but about elevating the whole team’s potential. As Kagami becomes a central point, Seirin’s teamwork improves, allowing Kuroko, Hyuga, Izuki, and the others to push beyond their limits.

Final Moments

With Kagami in the True Zone and Kuroko’s Misdirection Overflow, Seirin’s teamwork reaches its peak. Kuroko and Kagami lead the team with precision, allowing every player to contribute in both offense and defense. Their coordinated plays begin to unravel Rakuzan’s defense, putting them neck and neck with Rakuzan as the final minutes approach.As the game nears its climax, Akashi uses his Emperor Eye to its fullest, trying to maintain Rakuzan’s lead. However, Seirin’s team unity and resilience begin to overpower Akashi’s individual control over the game. In a final play, Kagami and Kuroko execute a perfectly timed sequence to bypass Rakuzan’s defense.In the last seconds, Kuroko sets up a pass for Kagami, who dunks over Rakuzan’s defense, securing the game-winning points. Seirin wins the Winter Cup by a narrow margin, marking their victory over the Generation of Miracles and Akashi.
